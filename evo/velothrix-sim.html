Species Profile — Velothrix aurantis

A slender, semi-aquatic ambush predator (~0.4 kg) inhabiting the brackish tidal marshes of Kel Nara. Males sport iridescent dorsal crests — brilliant in low-predator zones, muted near the deep channels where the Kelp Leviathan hunts by bioluminescent flicker. Females select mates based on crest brightness, but bright males suffer 2–3× higher predation. Lateral spines protect against gape-limited predators. Litter size is under Lack-type optimization. A coastal megapopulation continuously seeds inland marsh isolates via tidal corridor migration.
