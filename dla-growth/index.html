Diffusion-Limited Aggregation

DLA simulates how particles undergoing random walks (Brownian motion) stick together to form fractal structures. This process appears everywhere in nature:

  • Mineral dendrites — manganese oxide crystals growing on limestone
  • Electrodeposition — metal crystals forming on electrodes
  • Lightning — electrical discharge branching through air
  • Coral growth — calcium carbonate deposition patterns
  • Frost on glass — ice crystal formation

The beauty of DLA is that these complex, organic-looking structures emerge from the simplest possible rule: random walk + stick on contact.

How to use

  • Tap/click the canvas to place additional seed points
  • Presets change growth parameters for different visual effects
  • Seed shapes change the initial crystal nucleus geometry
  • Stickiness — probability a walker sticks on contact (lower = denser branches)
  • Walkers — more walkers = faster growth
  • Spawn gap — how far walkers start from the cluster edge

The fractal dimension of DLA clusters is approximately 1.71 — between a line (1) and a filled area (2). This self-similar branching appears at every scale.
